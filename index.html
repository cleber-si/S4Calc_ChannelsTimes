Waveplate sequence polarimetric only — one subcycle per position

16 positions → NSEQ = 16

Each active position is one subcycle. The waveplate steps between them, and that step costs 1.44 s of dead time; the cycle ends with a further 1.70 s. In photometry there is one subcycle per cycle and no waveplate.

Exposure times yours to set — target, sky, airmass, moon, saturation

NEXP is the safe knob: it cannot saturate anything.
What the numbers under each channel mean
wall
Wall-clock time this channel's sequence actually takes: its exposures plus every readout it has to pay for. With frame transfer off that is NEXP × (t_exp + read time), so a 0.5 s exposure at 1 MHz really costs 1.61 s.
idle
How long this channel sits doing nothing, waiting for the slowest channel to finish so the next subcycle can begin. The channel that sets the cadence has zero idle by definition — everyone else is waiting for it. This is the dead time you are trying to get rid of.
duty
The fraction of the sequence in which the shutter is actually open: (t_exp × NEXP) ÷ wall. Photons only arrive during that fraction. A duty of 45% means more than half the time is being spent reading the chip out rather than collecting light.
cadence
The length of one subcycle — the wall time of the slowest channel. All four channels are re-synchronised at each subcycle boundary, so the slowest one sets the pace for everybody.
